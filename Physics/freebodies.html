  1. When working with Free fall problems, we recommend acceleration (gravity), to be positive when the object's movement is up-down and negative when the object's movement is down-up.
    You may be asking why is that? Well, when you throw an object up, the gravity forces it down, through reducing the object's velocity by 9.8 m/s every second until it reaches zero.
    after the object's velocity is zero, the object will then come back you, gravity increasing the object's speed by 9.8 m/s every second until it comes back to your hand or earth's surface.

Introduction to two dimension free fall

Two dimension free falls involve movement of an object from both y-axis and x-axis perspective, meaning the object moves up and down, and left to right or right to left.
